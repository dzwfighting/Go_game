Go (game)

Go is a strategic game that uses a grid-like board and black and white pieces. Originated in China, in ancient China, there were many titles such as "Yi", "Ger", "Shoutan", etc. It belongs to the four arts of piano, chess, calligraphy and painting. It is called "Go" in the west

Chinese Chess Academy

On March 15, 1992, the founding ceremony of the Chinese Chess Academy was grandly held. The Chinese Chess Academy broke ground in October 1988 and was finally completed after more than three years. The Chinese Chess Academy is a public institution directly under the National Sports Commission. It is a comprehensive training base and competition venue for Chinese chess sports. It also directly manages the national team training tasks of the National Go Team, Xiangqi and International Chess, and undertakes various major domestic and international chess games. At the same time, various mass chess and card activities will be carried out.

In November 2019, the Chess and Cards Sports Management Center of the General Administration of Sport of China (Chinese Chess Academy) was awarded the protection unit of the national intangible cultural heritage representative project "Chess".

Chinese Go League

In the first year, only ten teams participated, with 18 games in two rounds of home and away games. After that, there were twelve teams in each competition, with 22 games in two rounds of home and away games. Each club can send 4 of the 6 players to participate. The players are not limited to players belonging to the Chinese Chess Academy. A large number of Korean and Japanese foreign players have appeared in the first and second leagues.

The bottom two teams in the first division are relegated to the second division of Chinese Go, and the top two teams in the second division are promoted to the first division. Since 2006, there has been a championship match between the league champions and the Korean league champions.
