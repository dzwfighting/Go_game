Go (game)

Nihon Kiin (Japanese: General Public Welfare Corporation Japan Chess Academy/いじんこうえきほうじんにほんきいん Ippankōekihōjin Nihon kiin) is the largest Go management organization in Japan and is responsible for organizing various chess games. Headquartered in Chiyoda, Tokyo, it also has central and Kansai headquarters in Nagoya and Osaka. Currently, there are about 300 chess players under the jurisdiction of Nippon Kiin.

Nippon Kiin was established in 1924 by the merger of the two largest Go organizations at the time, Honinbomon and Fangensha, with the support of Kishichiro Okura. After the establishment of the Japanese Chess Institute, it has successively competed with organizations or individuals such as Qizhengshe, Wu Qingyuan and Weiqi Xinshe. In 1950, some Kansai chess players headed by Hashimoto Utaro left Nippon Kiin and founded Kansai Kiin.

Nihon Kiin Title Tournament

The seven main tournaments of Go in Japan, according to the order of prize money, are Chess Master, Celebrity, Honinbo, Throne, Tengen, Gosei and Judan. Among them, the three tournaments with higher prize money, Chess Master, Celebrity, and Honinbo, are also called the "three title battles". Among them, Chess Master is the highest title. If you win these three titles at the same time, it is called the "big three crowns". There are many chess games where professional chess players compete for the championship, but what is the difference between the "Seven Great Chess Games" and other games for seven titles?

The biggest feature of the Seven Great Chess Games is that all players can participate. Of course, the way to decide the challenger is different. The celebrity game and the Honinbo game enter the circle after pre-selection. Entering the circle can prove that you are already a first-class chess player.

Prize money for the title race is as follows:

  • Chess master: 45 million yen
  • Tenth stage: 7 million yen
  • Honinbo: 28 million yen
  • Acer Saint: 8 million yen
  • Celebrities: 30 million yen
  • Throne: 14 million yen
  • Tianyuan: 13 million yen
